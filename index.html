1 Introduction

This tool processes transactions reports issued by the Mowali platform with the following objectives: ⁻ Keep track of the amounts due between DFSPs to settle their transactions. ⁻ Process settlement forex rate information received from the settlement bank/forex provider. ⁻ Prepare settlement files (sometimes also called reconciliation files) for DFSPs, summarizing the amounts they are about to settle with each DFSP they transact with. ⁻ Prepare "physical settlement", i.e. list all the payments and forex orders that should be sent to Mowali's partner bank(s) to settle a specific set of transactions. This extend to cases where the receiver's account is prefunded by the sender (aka pre-settled corridors). In that case, there is no "physical settlement" per se, but prefunding is managed in a similar way: the tool estimates the amounts necessary to prefund the next period and lists the corresponding payment orders to transfer these amounts from net senders to net receivers' accounts. ⁻ Issue all forex order files (and payment files in a future version). ⁻ Track each DFSP's liquidities, including liquidities in other currencies than their own (when they prefund receivers' settlement accounts) ⁻ Identify and isolates "abnormal" transactions (i.e. transactions with pending or inconsistent statuses) for reconciliation purposes. Based on the above, maintain a list of actions the business ops team should perform on Mowali's platform: operations on settlement ledgers, windows to be marked as settled once physical settlement has been completed… The batch of transactions to be settled is selected as follows: ⁻ For international corridors with a rate that changes every day: all transactions with a given rateSetID . ⁻ For single currency corridors OR corridors with a fixed rate that never changes (pegged currencies as EUR-XOF of EUR-XAF) OR pre-settled corridors: all transactions with a given windowID. At each settlement operation, business ops select the rateSetIDs and/or windowIDs they want to settle. The tool picks the corresponding transactions and performs the tasks described above for these transactions only - with one exception: the selection of abnormal transactions and the maintenance of their list applies to all the transactions found in the transaction reports. Some additional tools are also available to facilitate business operations, aside from the settlement process. They are described separately at the end of this document.
